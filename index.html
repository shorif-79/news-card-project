Anti-Corruption Measures

We need to rein in this corruption. If we don't rein in corruption, we will sink under it. If we can bring corruption down to zero per cent, the budget can be fully implemented.Corruption distorts institutions, deepens inequality, and undermines trust in public life. It can be overt or hidden, driven by personal gain or social pressure. Responses range from legal sanctions to civic engagement, and from institutional reform to shifting social norms. Addressing corruption effectively means understanding its forms, functions, and the systems that allow it to persist.
